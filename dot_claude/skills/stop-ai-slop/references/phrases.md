# Vocabulary Watch

Phrases and words that mark text as AI-generated. Load this when running the vocabulary watch (step 4 in priority order).

This list is *signal, not law*. Any individual word can appear in human writing. The mark of AI-slop is **density** — multiple flagged items per paragraph, repeated across sections.

## 1. English AI-favored words

## 3. English filler metaphors

Mechanical / techy metaphors used as decoration, not because they actually illuminate.

- "update your mental OS"
- "hack your life"
- "refactor your mindset"
- "level up your X"
- "10x your X"
- "rewire your brain"
- "the lens of"
- "moving the needle"
- "north star"
- "low-hanging fruit"
- "boil the ocean"
- "shift the paradigm"

Cut and write what you actually mean.

## 4. English adverb stacking

One adverb is fine; two in a row is AI emphasis-padding.

- "very, very important"
- "incredibly fast"
- "extremely effective"
- "remarkably consistent"
- "truly transformative"

Pick one stronger word. "Important" → "critical". "Very fast" → "fast" or give a number.

## 6. Japanese super-move coinages

Words that inflate a small observation into philosophy.

- 真理
- 虚飾
- 禁欲的
- 美学
- 境地
- 結末
- 宿命
- 営為
- 一義性
- 不可逆性

These are almost never warranted in a blog post or business writing. If used, they should land with weight, not as filler.

## 7. Japanese translation-ese verbs

Verbs that sound translated from English. Often paired with abstract subjects (see false agency in structures.md).

- 指している
- 示している
- 物語っている
- 体現している
- 結実している
- 醸成される
- 収斂する
- 浮き彫りにする
- 位置づけられる
- 役割を果たす
- 重要性が示唆される
- ことが明らかになった

Rewrite with the actual human agent and a plain verb.

## 8. Japanese adverb stacking

- 非常に
- とても
- かなり
- めっちゃ
- 普通に (as intensifier, "普通にすごい")
- ガチで
- マジで
- ぶっちゃけ

One per paragraph is the limit. Two adverbs back-to-back signals AI padding.

---

## 9. Japanese academic-tic words

Self-reference markers from academic writing, used out of context.

- 本稿
- 本記事
- 本書
- 筆者
- 当該

In a blog or business piece, these are misplaced. Use "この記事", "自分", "私" or drop entirely.

## 10. Japanese closing clichés

Endings that signal "the piece is over" via formula. See structures.md §13 for the fix.

- 今後の展開が注目されます
- 今後に期待です
- 続報が待たれます
- 動向に注目したいと思います
- これからが楽しみです
- 引き続きウォッチしていきます
- 興味深い動きと言えるでしょう

Cut, or replace with a specific next step.
